Teaching

I have taught multiple computing classes spanning various topics at primary, secondary, and university levels. In addition to the formal classroom, I have also mentored numerous undergraduate and masters students in a research capacity. For my teaching, I have received the Computer Science Department Teaching Award and nominated for the UChicago Physical Sciences Teaching Prize.

Computers for Learning

Computers for Learning at the University of Chicago covers technology, psychology, and pedagogy as they apply to educational technology so that students can design and build an educational learning application. Goals of this course include: learning education theory on how people learn, design principles for game design, learn how to create large-scale software in a team, and learn a technology in which to implement a learning application. I designed the programming assignments, where students built different elements of a game engine in Java. I also gave lectures on object-oriented programming and game engine architecture. I assisted in the instruction of this course for two terms.

Undergraduate Research

I have worked with over 15 undergraduates in various research projects; nearly all my papers are co-authored with undergraduate researchers. I mentored them in the development of artifact analysis programs, data analysis, both quantitative and qualitative, and technical writing.

Human-Computer Interaction

Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Virginia covers the evaluation of a system's usability, user and task analysis, conceptual models and metaphors, prototyping, and physical design of software user-interfaces. I mentored students on their independent projects, guided students at office hours, and aided in the development and evaluation of assignments. I assisted in the instruction of this course for one term.

Computer Architecture

Computer Architecture at the University of Virginia covers the organization of computer systems hardware, instruction set architectures, addressing modes, register transfer notation, processor design and computer arithmetic, memory systems, hardware implementations of virtual memory, and input/output control and devices. I led lab sessions, guided students at office hours, and aided in the development and evaluation of assignments. I also learned basic pedagogical techniques in computing from a companion couse. I assisted in the instruction of this course for two terms.
